Plundering Pirates

  • Technical Merit: What is interesting about your game technology?
    • Enemy ships spawn a certain distance away from the pirate ship so that it does not spawn right on top of the pirate ship and give free gold
  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype? What was the easiest or hardest part of making it?

  • High Concept: Run into the other ships to plunder as much gold as you can within the time limit!
  • Theme: Pirates
  • Mandated Variety: Move with arrow keys. Enemy ships spawn randomly. Timed high-score game.
  • Prototype Goal: What game mechanic is this prototype evaluating?
    • Moving, overlapping, randomly spawning
  • Player Experience Goals: What experience do you want players to have when playing your game?
    • I want them to feel excited enough to try and beat their previous score
  • Gameplay: You are the pirate ship. You move with the arrow keys. Your goal is to run into as many enemy ships as you can to increase your gold count before the timer runs out. Once the timer ends, you get your final score.
  • Strategies: No real strategies since the ships spawn randomly. You just have to hope they spawn really close.
  • Story/Setting/Premise: This is an abstract pirate game. This can be any pirate trying to plunder the seas for as much gold as they can steal.
  • Target Audience: Any ages
  • Play Time: 1 minute
