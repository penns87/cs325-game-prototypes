No devil lived on

  • Originality: Going backwards in time to work with your past self/selves (i was unaware that we were making playable games each week. thought we were making one game and iterating it. sorry for lack of actual gameplay)
  • Technical Merit: Creating other versions of the player that recreate the path they took, only backwards
  • Prototype Postmortem: Learned how to add background/assets/sprites. Hardest part was trying to figure out the proper syntax. Led to a few frustrating hours.
  • Prototype Assets: Obtained assets and code from here. Button asset obtained from here. Door asset obtained from here.
  • Prototype Closest Other Game: Obtained assets and code from here.
  • High Concept: Use the turnstile to work with your past self/selves in order to solve puzzles.
  • Theme: Things must only be added as the game progresses, not removed.
  • Mandated Variety: 2D platformer puzzle
  • Prototype Goal: For this prototype, using buttons to open doors
  • Player Experience Goals: Intrigue as they try to solve the puzzles
  • Gameplay: For this prototype, all you can do is move around with the arrow keys, use the character sprite onto a button to press it, and open a door when the button is pressed.
  • Strategies: Creating various paths that are helpful in some way for your future self, such as standing on a button in forward time, and standing on a different button in reverse time.
  • Story/Setting/Premise: Not sure if I'll ever be able to implement a story, but essentially you, the player, are trying to defeat a bunch of devil bosses by using time to your advantage (hence the name "No devil lived on" p.s. its also a palandrome). The devils are trying to take over the world and its up the the player to stop them.
  • Target Audience: Teens to adults
  • Play Time: As of now like 10 seconds.
